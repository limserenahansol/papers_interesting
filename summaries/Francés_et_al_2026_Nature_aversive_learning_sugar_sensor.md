## One-sentence takeaway

**Spaced (but not massed) aversive conditioning in satiated *Drosophila* transiently “re-enables” brain fructose-sensing neurons (Gr43a) so that ordinary post-training sugar intake can trigger a hormonal signal (thyrostimulin/Gpa2–Gpb5) that drives long-term memory (LTM) in the mushroom body—the same sensor pathway used after appetitive learning in hungry flies.**

---

## Background

- Internal nutrient sensors help assign value to food cues and regulate feeding.
- *Drosophila* has **four Gr43a-expressing neurons per hemisphere** that sense ingested carbohydrate (via fructose/Gr43a signalling) and promote feeding when animals are **fasted**.
- In **satiated** flies, those neurons are **silent** to sugar because **upstream dFB neurons** in the fan-shaped body release **tachykinin (Tk)** that disables Gr43a sensitivity.
- **Classic aversive olfactory conditioning** (odor + shock): one session gives short memory; **multiple spaced sessions** give **protein-synthesis-dependent LTM** lasting days, linked to **glucose-based metabolism** in **mushroom body (MB) Kenyon cells**. **Massed** training yields a different, shorter, more lipid-metabolism-dependent trace.

---

## Core discovery

### 1. Gr43a neurons are required for **aversive LTM** (not massed / not 3 h memory)

- Acute silencing of **brain** Gr43a neurons (Shi<sup>ts</sup>) for ~3 h **immediately after spaced training** abolishes **24 h LTM**.
- Shifting silencing to 3–6 h after training spares LTM: critical window is **right after learning**, when flies normally eat.
- Same silencing **after massed training** or **after single session** does not block the measured memory forms.
- If flies **cannot eat** during the consolidation window, LTM fails; **forced TrpA1 activation** of Gr43a neurons **rescues** LTM under food deprivation.
- **Sucrose or glucose** (not coconut oil) after training supports consolidation → **carbohydrate/fructose pathway**, not generic calories.

### 2. **Spaced training** restores fructose sensitivity of Gr43a neurons despite satiety

- In naive satiated flies, **fructose no longer excites** Gr43a neurons (calcium imaging).
- After **spaced associative** training, fructose response returns (**magnitude similar to fasted**); **unpaired** controls stay silenced like naive satiated flies.
- **Massed** training does **not** restore sensitivity.
- Sensitivity decays within ~**6 h**; delayed food access after 6 h prevents LTM.

### 3. Circuit: disinhibition of Gr43a via **reduced dFB activity** + **cholinergic FB.5/6**

- **Spaced training lowers ongoing calcium / “dFB activity”** in satiated flies (more **fasted-like**).
- **Artificial activation of dFB** right after spaced training **blocks LTM** (mimicking silencing Gr43a downstream consequence).
- **Muscarinic signalling (mAChR-B)** in dFB is needed for spaced training to shut dFB down and link to LTM.
- Connectome-guided candidates **FB.5/6** (cholinergic, presynaptic to dFB): **ChAT knockdown** or silencing impairs **LTM after spaced** but not massed/single training → experience-dependent **cholinergic inhibition** of dFB helps **re-open** Gr43a.

### 4. Output signal: **Gpa2–Gpb5 (thyrostimulin)** → **Lgr1 on MB neurons**

- Corazonin knockdown in Gr43a neurons does **not** block LTM; **Gr43a** receptor knockdown does.
- Gr43a neurons express **Gpb5** (and partner **Gpa2**): **RNAi against Gpb5 or Gpa2** in these cells **selectively impairs aversive LTM** after spaced training.
- **Thyrostimulin** acts on **Lgr1**; Lgr1 RNAi in **MB α/β** neurons phenocopies consolidation/metabolic readouts.
- Gr43a neurons **do not project to MB**; hormone-like signalling accounts for long-range effect.
- After spaced training, **early MB metabolic hallmarks** of LTM (pyruvate uptake in KC axons, glucose use via pentose phosphate pathway in somata) **depend on Gr43a and Gpb5** (links sugar sensor to known MB consolidation metabolism).

### 5. **Shared mechanism with appetitive LTM**

- After **appetitive** learning in fasted flies, Gr43a is already “on”; **silencing Gr43a / Gpb5** after reward also **impairs appetitive LTM**.
- Authors argue **thyrostimulin from fructose-sensing neurons to MB** is a **general consolidation signal** for **both** valences.

### 6. **Behavior: “fasted-like” feeding bias after spaced aversive training**

- **Spaced associative** (not massed, not unpaired) training **increases sucrose preference / intake** (FlyPad), in a **female** paradigm reported in figure legend.
- **Gr43a**, **Gpb5**, or **Lgr1** manipulations that disrupt LTM also **disrupt** this sucrose preference shift.
- Interpretation: same pathway couples **memory consolidation** to a **non-homeostatic feeding drive** (discussion link to **emotional eating**-like behavior in humans).

## Conceptual frame (“AND gate”)

**LTM consolidation after spaced training** is gated by **(1) Gr43a sensing capacity restored** AND **(2) actual sugar ingestion**—so consolidation is **formally subordinated to carbohydrate ingestion** even when the animal is **metabolically replete**. SpacingEffect ≈ transiently hijacking a **hunger-linked sugar sensor** for **non-feeding** memory.

---

## Why it matters

- Extends the job of **central nutrient sensors** from feeding/reward to **domain-general** **LTM** when learning statistics (**spacing**) engage the right circuit state.
- Unifies **aversive** and **appetitive** LTM through **one hormonal arm** onto MB.
- Suggests a **circuit substrate** for coupling **stressful or salient learning** to **increased sugar-driven behavior** without true energy need.
